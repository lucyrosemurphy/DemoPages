PLTW Cyber Security: Problem 2.3.1 E-Commerce Enrichment

Task Overview

"Bikes, Boards, and Beyond, a local retail shop, sells outdoor active gear, from skateboards and scooters to snowshoes and skiing equipment. The shop wants to grow its business and has decided to start an online shopping site to reach customers nationwide.

They have almost everything ready: a product database, a beautiful website design, extra inventory in their storage room, and a reliable shipping system in place. They have even decided, as part of their business expansion, to use an electronic system to track and manage in-store tasks and activities. They purchased digital scanners to track inventory in stock in the storerooms and on the shelves, new computers to process in-store orders and returns, and a badge scanning device to swipe employees in and out of the store to track their work hours.

Before Bikes, Boards, and Beyond goes live with their online shopping site, they need to test their website and ensure it is secure. Funneling their business through the world wide web could put the store's information and the information of their customers at risk. Therefore, the store has hired you and your cyber team to confirm that their site is secure and ready for, what they hope will be, high e-commerce traffic.

How will you secure their data? Do some data require more security and protection than others? What are some vulnerabilities that could threaten the security of the e-commerce site? How can you design a secure system, while ensuring the accessibility of the website to the customers and the store's internal data to its employees?"

Plan Of Action

IDENTIFY

  • Identify network architecture: Map out sever, firwall, and router configuration.
  • Try to identify possible vounerabilites in the HTML that would allow hackers to take advantage of exploits such as: SQL Injections and XXS
  • Examine packet data on wire shark to verify which avenues a potential hacker could take to gain unauthorized acess to information and/or data.

DETECT
  • Check the server software via the virtual desktop.
  • Ensure firewall is on and it's settings are appropriate. (Review CIA Triad)
  • Impliment security measures from previous lessons.
  • Preform tests simmilar to thoes a hacker might preform to test security:
    • Force directory browsing
    • Upload file to server from host machine
    • Cross Site Scripting (XXS)
    • Browse log files

PROTECT
  • Possible Threats and Solutions:
    • FTP rules -> Change firewall rukles
    • Log file name and location -> Move log files and change their name
    • SMTP -> Prohibit file transfer from unauthorized machines
    • Error Messages -> Create custom error pages
    • Directory Browsing -> Dissable directory browsing
  • Update the HTML: Ensure script tags cant run in message/text input boxes
